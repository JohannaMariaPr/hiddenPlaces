What is it about?

The Haus der Geschichte Österreich is Austria’s national museum dedicated to modern political and social history. Its exhibitions focus on the period from the founding of the First Republic in 1918 to the present day. Visitors can explore themes such as democracy, identity, migration, human rights, and Austria’s role in Europe. The museum places strong emphasis on individual stories and everyday perspectives alongside major political events. Its goal is to help visitors how Austria’s recent past continues to shape society today.

Highlight

The “This is Austria!?” station is one of the most engaging highlights. It invites visitors to explore how national identity is formed by language, culture, and everyday experiences. Through interactive elements, and short stories, the station shows that “Austrian identity” is not fixed but constantly evolving. It encourages visitors to reflect on what defines Austria today and how diverse perspectives shape the country’s self-image. This exhibit stands out for its mix of personal voices and cultural artifacts, offering a thoughtful look at what it means to belong in modern Austria.
